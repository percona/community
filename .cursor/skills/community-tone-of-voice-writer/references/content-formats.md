# Content Formats

Format guidance for each content type this skill produces. Read the relevant section
before writing.

---

## Web pages (Percona Community website)

**Purpose:** Orient, inform, and activate community members. Get to the point fast.

**Structure:**
- H1: Clear and specific. States what the page is for in plain language.
- Opening paragraph: 2-3 sentences. Answers "What is this page for?" immediately.
- Body: Short sections with subheadings. Scannable. No walls of text.
- CTA: One primary action per page. Specific, not generic.

**Length:** 150-400 words for most pages. Longer only for detailed technical contributor guides.

**Format rules:**
- Bullet lists for resources, links, steps, and options.
- Prose for anything that needs explanation or context.
- No more than 3 levels of heading.
- Every page must include at least one low-barrier first step for someone arriving cold.

**CTA examples (use this style):**
- "File a bug report with reproduction steps."
- "Ask your question on the forum — Percona engineers check in regularly."
- "Submit a PR against the GitHub repository linked below."
- NOT: "Get involved!" NOT: "Join us on our journey." NOT: "Upgrade to Pro."

## Blog posts

**Purpose:** Inform, teach, or take a position. Establish Percona as a genuine peer
in the open source community, not a vendor announcing features.

**Types:**
- **Technical how-to:** Solves a specific, named problem with real depth. Assumes competence.
  Shows commands, configurations, and real-world considerations.
- **Opinion / thought leadership:** Takes a clear position. Opens by challenging an assumption
  or naming a real problem. Backs the argument with examples. Ends with a challenge or question.
- **Community news / update:** What happened, what changed, what's next. No fluff.

**Structure:**
- Title: AP title case. "with" lowercase. Specific and honest. Not clickbait.
- Subtitle (optional): One sentence that adds context to the title.
- Opening: Hook in the first 2 sentences. No "In this post, we will..." preamble.
- Body: Subheadings every 200-300 words in sentence case. Mix of prose and bullets as appropriate.
- Closing: A question, challenge, or specific next step. Never a vague summary.

**Length:**
- Opinion pieces: 700-1,200 words
- Technical how-tos: 800-2,000 words depending on complexity
- Community updates: 300-600 words

**Format rules:**
- No "Introduction" as a heading. Just start.
- No "Conclusion" as a heading. End with something worth reading.
- Code blocks for all commands, queries, config snippets, and file paths.
- Subheadings in sentence case.

## Social media posts

### LinkedIn
- Max 150 words.
- Opens with a specific problem, a sharp statement, or a direct question. Not "Excited to share."
- No more than 3 hashtags at the end.
- Link to the relevant page, post, or forum thread.

**LinkedIn post structure:**
```
[Hook — 1 specific sentence]

[2-4 sentences of context, argument, or technical detail]

[CTA — 1 direct sentence]

[Link if applicable]

[#hashtag1 #hashtag2]
```

### Twitter / X
- Under 280 characters for standalone posts.
- Punchy. Can be a fragment or a direct question.
- Threads allowed for longer arguments. Each tweet must stand alone.
- No hashtags unless directly relevant.

### Mastodon / Fediverse
- Same rules as Twitter but skew more technical.
- The open source community is active here. Write for people who know their tools.

## Newsletters

**Purpose:** Keep community members informed. Written like a message from a person,
not a broadcast from a brand.

**Structure:**
- Subject line: Sentence case. Specific. Tells the reader what's inside.
- Opening: 2-3 sentences. What's happening right now. No pleasantries.
- Main content: 2-3 topics max. Short summary and a link for each.
- Community spotlight (optional): Name a contributor, a useful forum answer, a merged PR.
- Sign-off: A name where possible. "The Percona Community Team" if not.

**Length:** 300-500 words. Readers are busy.

**Format rules:**
- No "Dear Community Members." Start with what's happening.
- No "We hope this email finds you well." Just start.
- Each section: a heading, 2-4 sentences of copy, and a link.
- One primary CTA per newsletter.

**Subject line examples:**
- "MySQL 8.0 End of Life (EOL) is April 30 — here's your migration checklist"
- "Your first PR doesn't have to be perfect"
- "Percona Community update: April 2026"
- NOT: "Exciting news from Percona!" NOT: "Don't miss this!"

## Forum posts and community responses

**Purpose:** Answer questions, start discussions, welcome new members. Peer-to-peer.

**Rules:**
- Answer the actual question first. Context and caveats after.
- Link to documentation rather than reproducing it inline.
- If you don't know, say so and point to who might.
- When welcoming a new member: acknowledge their specific question, not a generic greeting.
- "Great question!" is banned. Just answer the question.
- If the question reveals a misunderstanding, correct it plainly without being condescending.
