[Chernihiv IT Cluster](https://chernihiv.it/en) ran **ProsvITa 2026** — a four-day educational intensive for students at Chernihiv Polytechnic National University. From June 22 to 25, engineers from local and international IT companies shared hands-on experience on topics that matter in production work today.

Percona has supported the Chernihiv IT Cluster for years. [Evgeniy Patlan](https://percona.community/contributors/evgeniy_patlan/) — Manager, Build and Release Engineering at Percona and a researcher at the university — has been part of that work since the beginning: meetups, university lectures, conference organizing committees, and curriculum updates alongside faculty. He spoke on the opening day, June 22, with two sessions for students.

## Program

The Cluster describes ProsvITa as an intensive for students who want a clearer picture of what the industry expects right now.

This year's program covered software development, pet projects for building a portfolio, cloud architecture, cybersecurity, IT management, and marketing. There was also a dedicated career track, including how to set up a LinkedIn profile that recruiters actually read. Sessions ran in person and online, so students could pick the format that worked for them.

## Evgeniy's talks

| Talk | Topic |
|------|-------|
| **Shift Left: Why Security Comes First, Not Last** | Building security into the pipeline from the start — release engineering, CI/CD, and supply-chain practices from real production work |
| **Commit to Your Career: Getting Started in Open Source** | How to find a first project, what a useful contribution looks like, and how open source work reads when you are starting out |

### Shift Left: Why Security Comes First, Not Last

Evgeniy's first session focused on shift-left security — treating security as a design constraint from day one, not a gate you bolt on before release.

That fits his day job. He leads Percona's build and release engineering team: packaging, CI/CD pipelines, release automation, and the kind of supply-chain hygiene that shows up in SBOMs and reproducible builds. Students heard how those practices connect to real production work, not just a checklist item at the end of a sprint.

### Commit to Your Career: Getting Started in Open Source

The second talk was about getting started in open source as a career move.

Evgeniy has walked this path himself — from build engineering to leading a release team at Percona, while staying active in the Chernihiv community. He has spoken at local conferences, contributed to university course modernization, and shown up at student events for years. The talk was practical: where to look for projects, what a useful first contribution looks like, and how open source work reads on a resume when you are just starting out.

## About Chernihiv IT Cluster

The Cluster was founded in 2017 and unites IT companies in the Chernihiv region. Its mission is to improve specialized education, promote Chernihiv as a location for IT business, and connect companies that share common values.

Programs like ProsvITa close the gap between lecture halls and production systems. Students meet engineers who ship software for a living and get a straight answer to questions that textbooks skip.

**Learn more:** [chernihiv.it](https://chernihiv.it/en)

If you are a student in Chernihiv, check [chernihiv.it](https://chernihiv.it/en) for the schedule and registration.

If you are elsewhere and wondering where to start in open source: pick a project you already use, find an issue labeled "good first issue," and send a PR. A clear bug report with reproduction steps counts too. That is how most of us got in.
