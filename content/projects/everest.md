## Percona Everest was great — and the story continues

If you tried **Percona Everest**, filed issues, shared ideas, or helped others in the community: thank you. Your feedback shaped a cloud-native platform for running PostgreSQL, MongoDB, and MySQL on Kubernetes — and that work is not stopping.

Percona Everest is transitioning into **OpenEverest** — an independent open source project with open governance and a growing community that goes well beyond a single vendor. The goal is simple: keep the platform healthy, trusted, and innovative by building it in the open, together.

## What changes — and what stays the same

**Open governance.** OpenEverest is meant to be shaped by maintainers, users, and partners across the industry — not by one company’s roadmap alone.

**Continuity for users.** If you rely on Percona Everest today, you can expect stability and support to continue through Percona’s enterprise offerings while the project opens up to broader collaboration.

**Percona stays in the game.** Percona remains an active contributor. **Solanica**, a Percona subsidiary, is focused on developing OpenEverest and growing its community.

**A platform built to grow.** OpenEverest is designed as an extensible foundation — more databases, more integrations, and closer ties to the cloud-native ecosystem over time.

## Where to go next

* Visit **[openeverest.io](https://openeverest.io/)** — try OpenEverest, explore the docs, and see how to get involved
* Learn about **[Solanica](https://solanica.io/)** — the team helping OpenEverest move forward
* Share feedback, contribute code or docs, or join roadmap discussions — the project succeeds when the community shows up
